# LinkedIn outreach: DS Interview Trainer

Use these for DMs and a post when inviting data scientist connections to try the app.

---

## Tasks

- [ ] **1. Set your landing URL** — Decide the link you’ll share (e.g. `https://yourapp.com` or `https://yourapp.com/signup`). Replace `[your-landing-url]` in the DM templates below.
- [ ] **2. Draft your post** — Pick Version 1 or 2 from “LinkedIn post” below. Optionally tweak for your voice.
- [ ] **3. Publish the post** — Post on LinkedIn. Add the landing URL in the **first comment** (so the algorithm doesn’t penalize the post).
- [ ] **4. List data scientist connections** — In LinkedIn: My Network → Connections. Search/filter by title (e.g. “Data Scientist”, “ML Engineer”) and note who you want to message (or work in small batches).
- [ ] **5. Send DMs in batches** — Copy one of the short DMs below. Personalize with first name and, if you like, one line on why you thought of them. Send to a few people at a time (avoid mass-sending in one sitting).
- [ ] **6. Follow up (optional)** — If someone tries the app, you can reply once to ask for one sentence of feedback. Don’t chase non-responders.

---

## Short DM (paste and personalize)

**Option A — direct and short**
> Hey [First name], I built a small tool for DS interview prep — questions that match your level, instant feedback, and it doubles down on the stuff you miss (A/B testing, causal inference, stats, ML). Free to start. If you're prepping or just want to stay sharp, would love your take: [your-landing-url]

**Option B — one sentence + link**
> [First name], I built an interview drill for data scientists (adapts to your level, instant feedback, spaced repetition on weak spots). Free — would love your feedback if you have 5 min: [your-landing-url]

**Tip:** Replace `[your-landing-url]` with your real URL (e.g. `https://yourapp.com` or `https://yourapp.com/signup`). Add one line about why you thought of them if you want (e.g. "Given your work in experimentation…") to keep it personal.

## LinkedIn post

**Version 1 — builder story**
> I kept wishing there was a DS interview drill that actually adapted to what I got wrong — so I built one.
>
> It’s called DS Interview Trainer: you get questions matched to your level, answer in your own words, and get instant feedback. It focuses on the areas you miss (A/B testing, causal inference, stats, ML, product sense) with spaced repetition so the practice sticks.
>
> Free to start, no fluff — just practice. If you’re prepping for interviews or want to stay sharp, I’d love for you to try it and tell me what you think. Link in comments. 👇

**Version 2 — shorter**
> Built a DS interview prep tool that adapts to your level and doubles down on what you miss — A/B testing, causal inference, stats, ML. Instant feedback, spaced repetition, ~30 min a day. Free to try. Link in comments if you want to give it a spin. 👇

**Tip:** Post the link in the first comment so the algorithm doesn’t penalize the post for an external link, and so people have to click through to your profile/comment.
